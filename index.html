01 About Me

Trained as an aeronautical engineer, I've spent my career close to the hardware: running CFD simulations, designing mechanical systems, and leading field operations for a government digitization project. I learned early that good engineering only matters if it actually ships. That meant tracking deliveries, managing delays, and keeping teams aligned, just as much as it meant solving technical problems.

That instinct is what's now pulling me toward management. I'm pursuing an MS in Engineering Management at Tufts University, formalizing what I was already doing informally: turning technical depth into program strategy. To prove the shift is real rather than theoretical, I've built a portfolio of PM artifacts, including a live analytics dashboard, product requirement documents, and data-driven case studies, using the same rigor I once applied to engineering problems.

What I'm looking for next is a Program or Product Manager role where technical fluency is an asset, not a translation problem: somewhere I can sit between engineering and strategy and make both sides move faster.
